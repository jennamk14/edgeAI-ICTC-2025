Overview

Edge AI—intelligence deployed on devices in the field instead of the cloud—is transforming wildlife monitoring and protection. From camera traps that recognize species in real time to acoustic sensors that flag poaching activity, edge computing enables rapid, autonomous conservation responses in places without reliable connectivity.

This full-day workshop (30-40 participants) will be the inaugural in-person gathering of the WILDLABS Edge Computing community and will showcase cyberinfrastructure for animal ecology from the NSF ICICLE Institute. Participants gain hands-on experience with edge AI tools, learn deployment strategies for resource-limited settings, and connect with a global network advancing this technology.

Workshop Snapshot

  • Title: Edge AI for Conservation: Deploying Intelligence Where It Matters Most
  • Duration: Full day (6-7 hours with breaks)
  • Participants: 30-40

Target Audience

  • Conservation technologists and practitioners using camera traps, acoustic sensors, or other monitoring devices
  • Animal ecology and wildlife biology researchers interested in automation
  • Technology developers building conservation tools
  • Protected area managers seeking cost-effective monitoring solutions
  • Anyone tackling conservation challenges in remote or connectivity-limited environments

Prerequisites: Basic familiarity with conservation technology (camera traps, acoustic monitoring). No programming experience required.

Materials & Requirements

Provided: Demonstration edge AI hardware (cameras, acoustic sensors, processing units); sample datasets; pre-configured software and models on loaner devices; ICICLE access credentials; printed quick-reference guides.

Participants bring: Laptop (recommended), optional projects or datasets, specific questions/use cases.

Venue needs: Tables for group work, reliable WiFi, power outlets, projector/screen, whiteboard or flipcharts.

Expected Outcomes

  • 30-40 practitioners gain foundational edge AI deployment skills
  • Strengthened WILDLABS Edge Computing community with defined priorities
  • Greater use of NSF ICICLE infrastructure by conservation technology teams
  • 3-5 working groups launched around specific edge AI applications
  • Shared repository of best practices and resources on WILDLABS

Relevance to ICTC Themes

  • Innovation & Intersections: AI, embedded systems, and conservation practice converge at the edge.
  • Access, Capacity, & Governance: Addresses the connectivity divide and builds community governance.
  • Applications & Impact: Real-world deployments with immediate conservation value.
  • Resourcing, Scaling, & Sustainability: Edge approaches cut bandwidth costs and leverage ICICLE at scale.

Why This Workshop Matters Now

Edge AI is at an inflection point: affordable hardware, increasingly accurate models, and urgent conservation needs demand real-time, autonomous monitoring. Knowledge gaps still slow adoption. This workshop connects practitioners, showcases infrastructure, and builds a community of practice ready to shape the future of edge AI for conservation.
